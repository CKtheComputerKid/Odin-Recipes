Honey Mead

Equipment

  • 1-Gallon glass jug
  • Large stainless-steel pot
  • Large stainless-steel spoon
  • Funnel
  • Sanitizer
  • Airlock and rubber stopper
  • Thermometer

Ingredients

  • 2.5 to 3 lbs, raw unprocessed honey
  • 0.5 package of wine yeast
  • Non-Chlorinated water
  • Optional, but recommended:
  • Fruits
  • Most berries work well. Particularly strawberries, blueberries, and black berries.
  • Apples, Oranges, Pears, Peaches, Mangoes, etc. etc.
  • It really is dealers choice here, it will take a few batches and experimenting before you find the flavor you're looking for!
  • Spices
  • Cloves, Cinammon, Nutmeg, Cardamon, etc. etc.
  • Much with adding fruits, it will take experimenting to find the right amount of spices for you.

Instructions

  1. Sanitize your equipment: pot, jug, funnel, airlocks, spoon, etc. Follow the directions on the sanitizer package.
  2. Heat .5 gallon of non-chlorinated water in the large pot on medium heat, do not boil. Add the honey and stir until it fully dissolves. Remove from heat.
  3. Add your chosen fruits and spices to the one-gallon jug.
  4. Using a funnel, pour the honey-water mixture into the one-gallon jug.
  5. Leave atleast 2 inches of headspace at the top. Add additional cold non-chlorinated water if needed.
  6. Secure the lid and shake well.
  7. When the temperature drops below 90 degrees F
    , add .5 of the yeast pack.
  8. Cap the bottle and give it a gentle shake to mix the yeast.
  9. Place the airlock on the jar, add a little water to the line, and then put the rubber stopper end into the jug.
  10. Wou should see bubbles within the first 48 hours.
  11. Store the jug in a dark cool place, or cover with with a towel. It will take 4 - 6 weeks for fermentation.
  12. It is ready when the bubbling has stopped or slowed to once every minute instead of every few seconds as it was initially.
  13. The liquid will be clear, and the yeast will settle to the jug's bottom.
  14. Do a taste test. Use a clean straw for tasting but be careful not to backwash into the mead.
  15. If you like the flavor, it is ready to bottle the mead.
  16. If it is too dry and you prefer a little sweeter, you can back-sweeten it. This is an additional process outside the scope of this recipe though.
